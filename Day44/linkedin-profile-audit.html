Senior Manager | Cyber Resilience & Disaster Recovery (BCP/DR) | Azure Cloud Infrastructure | HIPAA & ISO 27001 | FinOps | 17+ Yrs
Use this when you're actively job-hunting. It maximizes the odds you surface in a recruiter's Boolean search for "Disaster Recovery," "BCP," "Azure," or "ISO 27001" — search visibility over storytelling.
Option 2 · Value-proposition (for attracting opportunities)
I Design the Recovery Plan Before Disaster Strikes | Cyber-Resilient, Zero-Downtime Infrastructure Leader | Azure · FinOps | 17 Yrs
Use this once people are already landing on your profile (from posts, mutual connections, InMail) and you want the value to land instantly, not just the keywords.
Option 3 · Authority (for thought leadership)
Director-Track Infrastructure Leader | Building Zero-Downtime, Cyber-Resilient Enterprises on Azure | BCP/DR & FinOps | 17 Yrs
Use this once you're actively posting content in the DR/BCP/cyber-resilience space — it signals the aspiration and the niche authority you're building toward.
Given your goal mix (Director/VP first, thought leadership close behind), I'd start with Option 1 for search visibility, and switch to Option 3 once Day 2 and Day 5 posts (below) are live and you're building a content cadence. I also dropped "AI Enthusiast | Claude Learner" from all three headline options — not because it's not real, but because a Director-level headline has very few characters to work with, and every one of them should be pulling search or seniority weight. It fits better as a line inside your About or a future post, not competing with "Disaster Recovery" for headline space.
