3How This Was Researched

Expand methodology, per-criterion sourcing, and conflicts resolved ▶

Cost of Living

Pulled directly from Numbeo's Cost of Living Index by City (2026 dataset). Numbeo indexes everything against New York City = 100, based on user-submitted price data. Delhi, Gurgaon, and Noida are listed separately on Numbeo (there's no single "NCR" row), so the NCR figure used here is a simple average of those three city indices: (23.0 + 24.7 + 21.9) / 3 ≈ 23.2.

  • Pune: 22.8  |  Hyderabad: 21.7  |  Jaipur: 19.0  |  Delhi: 23.0, Gurgaon: 24.7, Noida: 21.9 (averaged for "NCR")

Air Quality / Pollution

Also from Numbeo's Pollution Index by City (current rolling dataset, Asia region table), which blends resident-perception survey data on air quality, water pollution, and related environmental factors. Same NCR-averaging approach applied: Delhi 90.4, Gurgaon 90.8, Noida 91.1 → averaged to ≈90.8. This roughly agrees with real-time AQI trackers (aqi.in, aqicn.org), which also consistently place Delhi-NCR far worse than the other three cities and Jaipur as the cleanest of the four.

Safety / Crime

Numbeo's Crime Index by City (2026), converted to their companion Safety Index (Safety = 100 − Crime), so higher = better across all four criteria in this tool. NCR again averaged across Delhi (40.9), Gurgaon (45.9), Noida (44.9).

IT Job Market — why it's flagged as an estimate

There's no single, universally agreed numeric "IT job market index" the way there is for cost of living or crime. Instead, multiple sources consistently agree on a relative ranking: Bengaluru is #1 nationally, followed by Hyderabad (#2) and Pune (#3), with NCR (Gurugram + Noida combined) sitting just behind as a major but fragmented hub, and Jaipur emerging as a fast-growing but much smaller Tier-2 hub. Concrete figures found: Hyderabad holds roughly 16% of India's IT job postings and Pune roughly 13.6%; Jaipur's IT sector has been growing at an estimated 25% year-over-year off a small base. Because no source gives NCR or Jaipur a directly comparable percentage figure, Claude converted this ranking into a 0–100 qualitative tier score (Hyderabad 90, Pune 85, NCR 80, Jaipur 40) to make it usable in the weighted model. This is Claude's own synthesis of the sourced rankings below, not a reproduced statistic — treat it as directionally reliable, not precise to the point.

Conflicts encountered

  • No source publishes a unified "NCR" city — Delhi, Gurgaon, and Noida are tracked separately with meaningfully different numbers (e.g., Gurgaon's cost index is notably higher than Delhi's). Averaging smooths this but hides real intra-NCR variation — if you're specifically choosing between Gurgaon and Noida, the gap matters more than this rollup shows.
  • Sources disagree on exact IT job-share percentages by a few points depending on methodology (job-posting counts vs. company headcount vs. IT-park square footage), but they agree on the ordinal ranking used here.
